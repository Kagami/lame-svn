In the great history of GNU naming, LAME stands for LAME Ain't an Mp3 Encoder.   LAME is not an mp3 encoder.  It is a GPL'd patch against the dist10 ISO demonstration source.  LAME is totally incapable of producing an mp3 stream. It is incapable of even being compiled by itself. You need the ISO source for this software to work.  The ISO demonstration source is also freely available, but any commercial use (including distributing free encoders) may require a license agreement from FhG.

LAME is an educational tool to be used for learning about MP3 encoding.  The goal of the LAME project is to improve the psycho acoustics, quality and speed of MP3.  Another goal of the LAME project is to use these improvements for the basis of a  patent free audio compression codec for the GNU project.

LAME v3.51 - November 1999:

  • MPEG1 layer III and MPEG2 layer III.
  • GPSYCHO: a GPL'd psycho acoustic model.  Variable bitrate (VBR), joint stereo,  great pre-echo control and much improved bit allocation!
  • Quality is substantially better than ISO psy-model based encoders and close to that of the FhG encoder.
  • Fast!   Encodes faster than real time on a PII 266.  Lower quality modes are much faster.
  • MP3x:  a GTK/X-Window MP3 frame analyzer for both .mp3 and unencoded audio files.
  • Download.  Compiles on GNU/Linux, Unix,  Windows, DOS, MacOS, BeOS, AmigaOS, OS/2

For those interested in MP3 encoding and LAME development, join the mp3encoder mailing list.

LAME was originally developed by Mike Cheng, (LAME v2.1f Nov 1998) and is now maintained by Mark Taylor.  It includes contributions from many people - see History.

LAME 3.x uses software from:

  • Decoding engine (for the frame analyzer):  mpglib, from the mpg123 project.  Mpg123 is an excellent  MPEG player.  Mpglib is released under the GPL.
  • The original ISO dist10 demonstration source . Also available in the "programmer's corner" on MP3Tech.
  • Input from almost any type of sound file now handled by the GPL'd libsndfile.

Free (speech) software which supports or uses LAME:

  • CDex:  ripper & encoder (for Microsoft Windows)
  • Grip:   gtk-based cd-player, ripper and encoder.  Supports cddb, cdparanoia and LAME.
  • Krabber  A KDE front-end ripper & encoder.
  • Mp3Maker  A WindowMaker enhanced front end to cdda2wav/cdparanoia and lame/bladeenc.
  • ripperX  GTK frontend for rippers and several encoders featuring CDDB support.
  • LiveIce  Real time streaming of mp3s.  Works with IceCast.

Free (beer) software which supports or uses LAME:

  • AutoLAMEr   Windows frontend for LAME.
  • LameBatch  Windows frontend for LAME.
  • RipChord   ripper and encoder for BeOS.
  • SecondSpin  ripper & encoder (for the Amiga)
  • ZLURP   CD-Ripper/Encoder/Tagger  (Windows, Mac, Linux)

The gold standard of encoders:

  • MP3Encoder  from FhG.  All other encoders strive to sound this good.  Demo version only encodes up to 30 seconds.

Other MP3 encoders:

  • BladeEnc  The most popular free encoder for Linux.  BladeEnc is a faster version of the ISO demonstration encoder with an improved user interface.
